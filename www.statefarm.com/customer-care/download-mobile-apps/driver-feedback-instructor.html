Driver Feedback™ Instructor Edition

State Farm® created Driver Feedback™ Instructor Edition (DFIE) with driver education instructors (and parent instructors) in mind. With DFIE, instructors can evaluate, record, organize, and monitor the progress of their student drivers. Encouraging smarter drivers is what this app is all about – and it's free!

  • Student drivers' trips are scored based on how they accelerate, brake and corner (ABCs) – the three riskiest behaviors.
  • Instructors can easily share student driver feedback and scores with parents.

What's New

  • Now able to import and export trips from one device to another.

Features

  • Record trips using your phones accelerometer to measure and score driving behavior.
  • Create and manage driver groups.
  • Enables instructors to give real-time feedback.
  • Captures driver's speeds throughout the trip.

Frequently Asked Questions(FAQs)

How much does the app cost?

The Driver Feedback Instructor Edition app is free and available in the Apple App Store and in Android Apps on Google Play.

Who can use the app?

This app was design for driving instructors, but anyone can use it! If you aren't a driving instructor, our traditional Driver Feedback app might be better for you. (Although parents with more than one young driver may find the multiple-driver features of the Instructor Edition useful.) You don't need to be insured with State Farm® to use this app.

Who sees student driving scores?

State Farm doesn't collect driver or trip information. An instructor may use the reporting feature to share student trip information with a parent or guardian by email. (See "How do I use the app?") The data recorded by this app will not impact insurance rates.

What does the app measure?

  1. Each trip results in an overall trip score and individual scores for three key driving behaviors. We call these the ABCs of driving:
    A - Acceleration
    B - Braking
    C - Cornering
  2. The app displays any "ABC alerts" color-coded yellow (slight instruction recommended), orange (moderate instruction recommended) and red (significant instruction recommended).
  3. The app scores vary, depending on the type of road and traffic congestion.

How do I use the app?

Record your student driver trips to track their progress and help them visualize specific areas for improvement. A higher score means better driving. You can easily share student driving results from the app with a parent or guardian by email. This can help adults encourage their student drivers and focus on practicing specific skills.

How do students improve scores?

  • Encourage students to download the Driver Feedback app to log their own practice drives and track their progress toward safe driving.
  • Encourage students to reduce red (significant), orange (moderate), and yellow (slight) alerts from future drives.
  • As students master slow and smooth acceleration, braking and cornering, they will see their scores increase.

What if I get a phone call or text while I am recording a trip?

Please follow all federal, state, and local laws regarding phone usage in moving vehicles. Taking calls or answering texts while recording a trip may impact the trip score.

Why do I have to lay this flat or use my cup holder?

We are using a device's accelerometer to collect driving data on acceleration, braking, and cornering. We must calibrate this technology to provide an accurate score. Normally, you can place your phone in your dash mount (with a slight change in the angle of the mount), use your cup holder or lay it flat in a secure location.

Why did State Farm create this app?

We believe if we can provide detailed information about driving behaviors, it may help driving instructors document and improve the rate of learning for students in their driver training programs. The Driver Feedback Instructor Edition makes it easy to involve parents, which can help the learning process. If we can help create safer drivers, our roads will be safer for everyone.
