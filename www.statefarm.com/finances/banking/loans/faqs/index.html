State Farm Bank® Loan FAQs – Vehicles

Here are answers to some Frequently Asked Questions about vehicle loans from State Farm Bank.

State Farm Bank deposit accounts are FDIC insured.

Talk to an Agent

Have more questions? Find a local State Farm Agent here

Before Applying for a Loan

What types of vehicles can I finance with State Farm Bank?

Automobiles, Light Trucks, SUVs & Vans:

  • New & Used
  • Terms as long as 84 months (for new models)

Boats, Motor Homes & Travel Trailers:
  • New & Used
  • Terms as long as 240 months (for new models)

ATVs*, Jet Skis*, Motorcycles, Snowmobiles*, & Other*:
  • New & Used
  • Terms as long as 72 months (for new models)

Certain vehicle collateral restrictions may apply.

*Certain collateral types may not be acceptable in all states. Please contact your State Farm Agent or State Farm Bank at 877-SF4-Bank.

What types of loans do you offer?

Dealer Purchase
  • Used when the vehicle is being purchased from a licensed dealership.

Lease Buyout
  • Used when a leased vehicle is being purchased. Financing available to the Lessee only.

Refinance
  • Used when a customer currently has a vehicle loan through another financial institution and would like to refinance it through State Farm Bank.

Personal Secured
  • Used when the customer has a vehicle title with no liens and is pledging the vehicle as collateral for a loan.

Private Party
  • Used when the vehicle is being purchased from an individual.
  • When State Farm Bank is not the lien holder, specific procedures exist for handling loan requests for a private party purchase. Please contact your Agent for assistance with information that is needed to complete these private party sales with an existing lien transaction.

Prequalification Application
  • Used when a customer is looking to see if they qualify for financing with State Farm Bank. The customer would like an approximate amount and annual percentage rate.

What is a simple interest loan?

Simple interest is a way of allocating monthly loan payments between the principal and interest. The amount of your loan payment that is allocated to interest is based on the unpaid portion of the principal balance, the interest rate on your loan, and the number of days since your last payment. For example, if we receive a payment 20 days since your last payment, you will then be charged 20 days of interest on the unpaid principal balance of your loan. The rest of your payment is credited to the principal which reduces the unpaid principal balance on your loan.

How do I apply for a vehicle loan from State Farm Bank?

Just speak with your local State Farm agent, team member or representative and they'll assist you.

What information do I need to apply?

During the loan application process, you will need to provide your:
  • Name
  • Address
  • Social Security number
  • Collateral information
  • Employment and income information

The same information will be needed from a co-borrower or a co-signer.

In which states is vehicle financing available?

Vehicle loans are only available in the United States. Within the United States, automobiles are eligible for financing in all states except for Massachusetts and Rhode Island.

If I can get special rate financing at the dealership, why would I finance my car with State Farm Bank?

  • Fixed rate loans
  • Annual Percentage Rate (APR) and approval are good for 30 days from date of approval
  • Payoff Protector® included1 (not an insurance product)
  • May be able to include sales tax as a part of the amount borrowed
  • Convenience of applying at an agent's office
  • No application fee
  • Interest rate reduction for customers who elect to deduct their monthly payment using automatic payment through a State Farm Bank deposit account (checking, money market or savings) at closing2

Is there a required down payment for a vehicle loan?

Down payments are evaluated on an individual basis.

Why should I refinance my current vehicle loan with State Farm Bank?

  • There is a title fee charged by the Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) to record the title with State Farm Bank as the lien-holder; however, State Farm Bank's vehicle loans have no direct closing costs. Often customers find that they can quickly recoup the cost of the title recording fee due to the savings in their monthly payment.
  • State Farm Bank vehicle loans include Payoff Protector1. Payoff Protector will cancel the difference between the amount your auto insurance pays on a total loss claim and the principal balance you owe on your vehicle loan, subject to certain restrictions such as if your account is not in good standing. You must also continue to make monthly payments while waiting for your insurance claim to settle in order to be eligible.
  • State Farm Bank rates are competitive.
  • Your monthly payment on a State Farm Bank vehicle loan may be lower than the dealer, even if the dealer has a slightly lower interest rate based on there being no application fee, the removal of GAP insurance costs, or a longer loan term from State Farm Bank.

Switching your current vehicle loan to State Farm Bank may save you money each month. Talk with a State Farm agent to find out more.

How much can I afford to spend?

Visit this loan calculator to help you estimate how much you might be able to afford each month.

How can I estimate my monthly loan payments?

Visit this vehicle loan calculator for an estimation of your loan payments.

Is there an application fee?

No.

What is minimum/maximum amount of a loan?

The minimum is $5,000. Depending on the collateral, the maximum is up to $250,000.

Must I be a legal citizen to apply for a vehicle loan?

Applicant must be 18 years of age or over and a U.S. citizen, resident alien or non-resident alien and residing in the U.S.

Can I be approved before I choose my car?

You can apply for a "pre-approval". This is a preliminary approval based on your credit report, application information and debt-to-income ratio.

Required information:
  • Collateral type
  • Collateral year

Final approval cannot be provided until specific collateral is submitted and Bank underwriting is completed.

How long is an approved interest rate and loan offer valid?

An approved interest rate and loan offer are good for 30 days after the date you were approved.

What are the vehicle loan eligibility requirements and conditions?

General Eligibility
  • Applicant must be at least 18 years of age
  • At least one borrower must be listed as a registered owner on the title/registration
  • Only one physical piece of property may secure the loan
  • Minimum loan term is 12 months
  • Minimum loan amount is $5,000
  • Minimum monthly payment is $100
  • Maximum loan amount of $250,000
  • Insurance is required (can be from any insurer acceptable to State Farm Bank)
  • Proof of identity is required

What types of vehicles are not eligible for a loan from State Farm Bank?

  • Vehicles for hire
  • Custom built collateral
  • Salvaged, rebuilt, reconstructed, and gray market vehicles do not qualify
  • Boats, motorcycles, recreational vehicles and any collateral considered "other" for the loan type are not eligible for cash out or personal secured loan requests
  • Mileage restrictions: 150,000 miles maximum or driven more than 50,000 per vehicle year
  • Vehicles used as primary residence
  • Additional vehicle collateral restrictions may apply

Collateral Type Eligibility Requirements
Boats No collateral older than 15 years, no offshore or drag racing boats
Motorcycles No collateral older than 15 years, no used sports bikes (including suffixes R and RR).
Recreational Vehicles No collateral older than 15 years

Your Vehicle Loan Questions

How do I set up Automatic Payments?

Complete and mail this form [PDF-649.5KB] along with a voided check or savings deposit slip to State Farm Bank:
Attn: Loan Services
State Farm Bank, F.S.B.
P.O. Box 5961
Madison, WI 53705

If you are paying with a State Farm Bank deposit account, you don't need to send a voided check or savings deposit slip.

What payment alternatives do I have, in addition to mailing my payment every month?

State Farm Bank offers the following options to initiate payments on a Vehicle Loan:

Automatic payment via ACH (Automatic Clearing House)
  • Uses a non-State Farm Bank account (external bank)
  • No fee charged by State Farm Bank

Automatic payment via Auto Pay
  • Uses a State Farm Bank checking, savings, or money market account
  • Discount received on loan's interest rate if set up at time of loan closing2
  • No fee

Mail (Coupons)
  • No fee for standard mail
  • Fees may apply by carrier for expedited mailing options

MoneyGram
  • Fees assessed by MoneyGram may apply

Online at statefarm.com®
  • For accountholders with a registered user ID and password
  • No fee

Phone Payment
  • Via IVR (Interactive Voice Response)
    • No fee if State Farm Bank deposit account is used
    • $10 fee applies for external accounts
  • Representative assisted phone payments
    • No fee if State Farm Bank deposit account is used
    • $15 fee applies for external accounts
    • No fee for principal only payments

Transfer from a State Farm Bank deposit account to the Vehicle Loan account
  • No fee

Transfer from a State Farm Bank HELOC account to the Vehicle Loan account
  • No fee

Western Union
  • Fees assessed by Western Union may apply
  • Only payment option where a credit card can be used

Wire transfer
  • Service fees may apply from bank that wire is initiated from

1State Farm Bank Payoff Protector is automatically included as a provision of all newly issued vehicle loan promissory notes. Payoff Protector is not an insurance product. Subject to the terms, conditions, and restrictions of the Payoff Protector provision in your State Farm Bank Promissory Note and Security Agreement. Your loan must be in good standing and you must continue to make your monthly payments until your insurance claim is settled in order for Payoff Protector to apply. If your vehicle is determined to be a total loss before the loan is paid off, State Farm Bank will cancel the difference between the insurance payout and the unpaid principal balance due on the loan. Please contact State Farm Bank for additional details.
