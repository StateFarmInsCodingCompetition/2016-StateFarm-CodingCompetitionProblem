Welcome to the State Farm Bank® Security Center

State Farm Bank takes your online security very seriously. Here in the Bank Security Center, you can learn how we actively work to keep all your personal information and data safe, and how you can take steps to protect yourself from identity theft, fraud, and online scams.

State Farm Bank deposit accounts are FDIC insured.

Protecting You

  • State Farm® uses 128 bit encryption technology to keep your personal information private.
  • Knowledge Based Authentication (KBA) is used as a part of our customer verification process.
    • We use an industry-leading provider who specializes in identity verification for companies, healthcare and government entities.
    • Verification questions are generated randomly during each customer interaction, making it impossible to determine which questions will be presented in a quiz.
    • The provider pulls answers for the verification questions from a wide variety of customer information databases, but not from State Farm customers' personal information.
    • This approach has become a best practice in the industry and will help to keep your information safer from those who might want to illegally access it or use it for fraudulent purposes.
  • If your State Farm Bank® Visa® Credit or Debit card is lost or stolen, call us immediately, toll-free:
    • For debit cards, 877-SF4-BANK (877-734-2265). If you are outside the United States, call us collect at 309-994-6170.
    • For credit cards, 877-SF4-VISA (877-734-8472). If you are outside the United States, call us collect at 706-644-0852.
      ***Callers who are hearing or speech impaired should dial 711 or use a preferred Telecommunications Relay Service (TRS).
  • For more information on how to dispute a charge, please see How do I Dispute a Charge?
  • Visa Zero Fraud Liability: You are not responsible for unauthorized transactions processed through the Visa network on your State Farm Bank® Visa® Credit or Debit card.
    ***Does not apply to ATM transactions or PIN transactions not processed by Visa.  Cardholder must promptly notify State Farm Bank of suspected fraudulent transactions.
  • State Farm Bank monitors your account for unusual or fraudulent activity and has procedures in place to contact you whenever this type of activity is identified.
  • To help prevent unauthorized account usage, your online session will time out after 15 minutes of inactivity. If time out occurs, you must log back into your account.

Your Identity

Protect Your Identity

  • Keep your Social Security Card in a secure place. Don't keep it in your wallet or purse. Avoid using it as a form of identification. If asked to provide your social security number (SSN), be sure you know why. Never have your driver's license or SSN printed on checks—write it in if necessary.
  • Watch the mail. Stolen mail can provide thieves with critical information, so put your mail on hold with the postal service if you're not going to be home for any length of time. If you pay credit card and loan bills by mail, never put the full account number on your check—use the final four numbers.
  • Shred it. Invest in a shredder. Shred anything with personal information before you throw it away. Besides credit card solicitations, shred things like ATM receipts, bank, credit and loan statements, receipts, and utility bills. Also be sure to shred old credit cards before disposing.
  • Beware of phony phone calls. Never offer personal information of any kind over the phone if you did not make the call. Thieves often use high-pressure tactics to get this kind of personal data. Just hang up.
  • Keep your information current. Always notify your bank and other credit issuers if your address or phone number changes. It is important to have the most current contact information on file in the event that you need to be contacted for suspicious account activity.

Social Media

  • Don't reveal too much information. Be cautious about how much personal information you provide on social networking sites. The more information you post, the easier it may be for someone to use that information to steal your identity and access your accounts.
  • Use privacy and security settings. Social networks allow you to change your privacy settings. They are there to help you control who sees what you post and manage your online experience in a positive way.

Fraudulent Email and Phishing

  • What is phishing? Phishing means sending fraudulent email messages that ask for confidential information. The information is then used to gain access to a victim's accounts and steal their identity.
  • We'll never ask for confidential information. State Farm Bank places a high priority on helping you protect your private information, and will never initiate a request for this type of information via email. We work with the authorities to shut down fraudulent websites upon learning of a phishing attempt.
  • Receive a phishing attempt? If you receive an email asking for personal information and it appears to be from State Farm, statefarm.com, State Farm Bank or has the State Farm logo, do not respond. Keep in mind:
    • State Farm Bank will not send emails containing attachments or require customers to send personal information via email or pop-up windows.
    • State Farm Bank will send customers an email notification when a message is waiting in their secure online inbox.
  • Red flags:
    • A phishing email will usually contain a generic greeting.
    • Some phishing emails look exactly like they came from a credible source. A credible source will never ask you for your personal or account information via email. Call the source to verify.
    • These emails are usually urgent requests.
    • Many emails will often contain numerous misspellings and grammatical mistakes.
    • Don't respond to an email that asks for you to provide your personal information to verify or re-activate an account you have.
    • Avoid opening links through emails as they may contain viruses.
  • Do not reply to unsolicited emails. If you have questions about your account, you should contact State Farm Bank directly at 877-SF4-BANK (877-734-2265).

Reporting Identity Theft

Think you're a victim of identity theft? If you believe you are the victim of identity theft, we recommend you contact us toll-free at 877-SF4-BANK (877-734-2265). The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) provides a comprehensive website on how to report identity theft. You can also call them at 877-IDTHEFT (877-438-4338).

Devices & Accounts

Financial Cards

  • Sign all new cards immediately.
  • If your State Farm Bank Visa® Credit or Debit card is lost or stolen, call us immediately, toll-free:
    • For debit cards, 877-SF4-BANK (877-734-2265). If you are outside the United States, call us collect at 309-994-6170.
    • For credit cards, 877-SF4-VISA (877-734-8472). If you are outside the United States, call us collect at 706-644-0852. ***Callers who are hearing or speech impaired should dial 711 or use a preferred Telecommunications Relay Service (TRS).
  • Only include the last four digits of your credit card account number on your check payment.

PIN Numbers

  • Do not share your Personal Identification Number (PIN) with anyone else.
  • If you must write down your PIN, do not keep it in your wallet, purse, or on the card itself.
  • When selecting a PIN, avoid numbers and letters that can be easily identified or associated with you. Do not use your initials, birth date, or telephone number.
  • Periodically change your PIN Number.

Staying Safe While Shopping Online

  • Review your order confirmations, bank and credit card statements in a timely manner.
  • Never make online financial transactions with unfamiliar websites or institutions. Many thieves set up fake sites to steal information. Shop with merchants you know and trust.
  • Be sure to log off completely from any website after an online transaction.
  • Make certain the website is secured. Only provide your account information when the browser indicates a secure or encrypted connection. An encrypted connection is normally indicated by an "https://" in your browser's address bar in front of the address of the page you are viewing.
  • Look for a secured symbol at the bottom of your web browser. The secured symbol is most often displayed as a padlock.
  • If you do not receive a statement or you receive it late, opened, or tampered with, contact State Farm Bank immediately at 877-SF4-BANK (877-734-2265).

User ID and Password Security

  • Change passwords every one to two months.
  • Use different user IDs and passwords for your bank accounts and other websites, including social media.
  • Keep your security software and operating system up to date so you have the latest security patches.
  • Always use strong passwords. Strong passwords:
    • Are at least eight characters long (14 or more recommended, consider using a "passphrase")
    • Contain both uppercase letters and lower case letters
    • Contain numbers and/or special characters (&$@*, etc.)
    • Do not contain your user name, real name, or company name
    • Do not contain a complete word, in any language
    • Are significantly different from previous passwords

Device Security

  • Use a PIN to restrict access to any mobile device.
  • Don't "root" or "jailbreak" your mobile device by removing hardware restrictions imposed by your device's operating system in order to gain root access to its file system and manager. This makes it more vulnerable to malicious software.
  • Utilize automatic screen-locking on your mobile device.
  • Enable remote wipe and location features on your mobile device. This will help to ensure that your personal information is protected if your mobile device is lost or stolen.
  • Only download apps from trusted sources such as iTunes or Google Play.
  • Keep your anti-virus software up to date. Out of date software could allow malicious information onto your device.

Fraud & Scams

Protect Yourself from Scams

  • Do not provide your personal/private information — including information about your insurance policies, financial accounts, or credit cards — to any person unknown to you or with unverified credentials or connections to you.
  • Do not try to cash any unknown or unauthenticated checks, or otherwise accept any offer of money.
  • If the State Farm name or logo is used in a communication and you are suspicious, call a local State Farm agent who can help you determine whether what you received is authentic.
  • If you receive any communication that looks different from what you normally receive from your agent or State Farm, don't hesitate to call your agent or an authorized company representative to report your suspicions.
  • If you are contacted by phone or in person, be wary of anyone who gives you only vague information about themselves or the entity they claim to represent. Ask questions. Try to get as much information as possible. If the caller gets nervous, gives sketchy or little information, or refuses to provide information, it is likely a scam.
  • As society invents new ways to communicate and conduct business, consumers are targeted by scams through a variety of channels. Scam artists are getting more creative and sometimes use the names of large, reputable companies to make their scams convincing.
  • Consumers, including State Farm customers, are randomly targeted by scam artists who have used our company name to lure their victims. State Farm is concerned about these scams, and whenever possible, we provide the information we receive about them to the proper authorities to help in their investigations.

Learn more about identifying and reporting possible scams in the State Farm Security Center.

Social Engineering

  • Social Engineering is the concept of obtaining personal information direct from individuals.
  • It is much easier for a thief to ask for your personal information (i.e. password, Social Security Number, PIN number etc.) than it is to hack this information.
  • A common form of social engineering is phishing. Slow down, research the facts, and think about what and who you are sending your information to.

ATM Scams/Safety

ATM theft is a growing problem. As ATM technology advances, so does the technology used by those attempting to illegally obtain money from your account. Listed below are some common ATM Scams:

  • A thief uses an external blocking device to disallow your card from coming out of the ATM once inserted. The thief will then attempt to obtain your PIN number. This may happen from afar, or someone may approach offering assistance while having you enter your PIN. If the thief is successful, they will have your card and your PIN number.
  • Card Skimmers: Card Skimmers are attached to the ATM and can read your card and obtain all of its information, including account number, balance, and PIN number. Once armed with that information, a thief could use your account information to make fraudulent purchases.

ATM Transaction Safety

  • Be wary of people trying to help you with ATM transactions.
  • Do not use an ATM if you observe or sense suspicious persons or circumstances.
  • Fill out your deposit slip before arriving at an ATM location.
  • Avoid large cash withdrawals.
  • Do not count or visually display any money you received from the ATM, until in a safe place to do so.
  • Take all of your receipts with you.
