Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers.

Here are our frequently asked questions about applying for jobs and working at State Farm®.

Frequently Asked Employment Questions

Is State Farm® hiring?

Yes. There are positions available for all levels across the country.

Explore our Career Areas to learn about our wide range of opportunities. Visit our Search Jobs page to find current job openings.

What do you look for in a new employee candidate?

Qualifications vary because of the range of career opportunities available. We look at multiple factors when considering candidates, including:
  • Competencies
  • Skills 
  • Experience
  • Education
  • Professional background

What are the hours of operation at State Farm?

Hours of operation vary between the position, department, and type of job. Many departments operate 24 hours a day, seven days a week to meet the needs of our customers.

Does State Farm offer benefits?

Yes, our Total Rewards employee benefits package offers cash and non-cash rewards to full and part-time employees in exchange for their contributions to our success. Visit the Employee Benefits page for more details.

Does State Farm offer competitive compensation?

Compensation varies based on position and is reviewed annually to ensure it remains competitive in the job market.

Compensation is often listed on the job posting, but may also be discussed with the HR recruiter during your interview.

Does State Farm offer internships?

Yes. Visit the Become an Intern page to learn about our internship program

To find internship openings, follow these steps:
  1. Click Search Jobs.
  2. Click Full/Part Time.
  3. Select Internship.

How do I contact a recruiter?

A recruiter will contact you if you are considered for an interview.

To check the status of your application, follow these steps:
  1. Log into your Careers account.
  2. Click My Career Tools. 
  3. Select My Active Applications.
    • A list of your job applications and their current status will display.

Do I need to have an insurance license to work at State Farm?

Some positions do require licensing. A recruiter will discuss licensing requirements during the interview process.

If you have an existing insurance or financial services license, the license would be reviewed to confirm it meets the requirements for the position.

What kind of new employee training and career development opportunities does State Farm offer?

We make employee development a priority. We offer formal and on-the-job training. The length and type of training is based on position. Additionally, our Job Opportunity Program (JOP) allows employees to compete for opportunities in the organization.

The Employment Process

Where can I get more information about the application and hiring process?

Visit the Employment Process page for additional information.

How will I receive information and updates during the employment process?

Email is the primary way we'll communicate with you, although recruiters may contact you by phone. Choose an email address you check regularly and one that can receive confidential information (e.g., pre-employment testing, interview confirmations, and job offers). Emails may be delivered to your spam or junk folders.

In order to receive all communications, keep the contact information in your applicant profile current.

Is there pre-employment testing?

You'll receive assessment information by email if the position you applied for requires pre-employment testing.

What is the interview process?

The interview process varies based on position. The initial interview could be with Human Resources or the hiring manager and may take place in-person or over the phone.

Note: Professional attire is encouraged for all in-person interviews.

Are background checks and drug tests part of the hiring process?

Yes. Job offers are contingent on a personal background check and drug screen. If your background check and drug screen produce satisfactory results, your offer will be confirmed in writing.

How long should I expect to wait for a decision after an interview?

Although timeframes vary based on position, our goal is to make a prompt final decision once all candidates have been interviewed. A recruiter will provide details on the position you are seeking.

If I am selected for a position, what documents should I bring on my first day?

The Human Resources department is obligated by law to verify a new employee's identity and work eligibility within the first three days of employment. A recruiter may discuss this and other new employee questions with you at the time of the job offer. New employees receive a copy of Form I-9, which lists acceptable documents to bring to establish your identity and work eligibility.
