Become an Agent
From Military to Agent

Leaders Needed

It's a simple fact. We need you. This is the opportunity transitioning service men and women can embrace.

Align yourself with a stable, well-known company and get the opportunities you need, the freedom to make your own decisions and the chance to earn a living, all while continuing your mission to serve others.

Contact a Recruiter

Never Stop Leading the Way

Leaders never stop leading, and that's why as a State Farm agent you can continue to assess challenges, strategically direct your staff to meet those objectives, and drive your career to meet your personal goal — all while helping others to achieve their goals.

Position yourself for success with a company that:

  • Has led the insurance industry since 1922
  • Is ranked in the Fortune 500 Top 50
  • Is continually recognized for financial strength, community involvement and customer service

Serve and Protect

As a State Farm agent, you'll be serving the nation's citizens on an individual level, face-to-face, right in the neighborhood where you live, helping customers attain and manage their personal needs, cares and dreams.

  • Support and protect our nation's families
  • Sustain the good in America's neighborhoods
  • Develop a lifestyle where work, life, values and goals are in sync
  • Build a rewarding, fulfilling career where you can work for something you believe in

Here For You Because You Were There For Us

State Farm® supports you in this opportunity with competitive benefits, which may include personal assistance, relocation, financial assistance, training and more. We'll help you navigate potential roadblocks to your success, so you can achieve on the accelerated level you're used to.

Support may include:

  • The opportunity to sign with a "G.I. Jobs rated top 2% Military Friendly Employer"
  • Lump sum payment: $4,000 renters; $10,000 homeowners
  • Movement of household goods
  • Temporary storage (if needed) for up to 90 days
  • Home marketing and home finding assistance
  • Spousal employment assistance
  • Training and development
  • Marketing assistance
  • Strategic leadership development training
  • Administrative services support
  • Access to claims, underwriting and public affairs resources
  • Agency/Sales resources are available for things like compensation, licensing, and incentive support
  • Banking, deposit and loan support
  • 24/7 customer response center support
  • Around-the-clock computer and IT support
  • Market securities and financial services product support
  • Agent mentoring and guidance programs
  • Signing bonuses
  • Product commissions
  • Production incentives
  • Travel around the world for qualifiers
  • Start-up and expense management assistance
  • No experience necessary to become a State Farm agent
  • Continue to lead the way, your way

Excellence in the Heart of Service

Doing what's right is in your nature. It's a source of passion and pride. It's inherent in your values as a person of service. At State Farm, we strive for that same level of excellence.

It's about meeting difficult challenges head on — about understanding the needs of the neighbors we serve and operating on an accelerated level to help them meet their needs and goals. It's being committed, and being there for them in times of need — large and small.

That's who you already are and that's what State Farm agents do.

A Career Edge Like No Other

Personal strength, a strategic mindset and the commitment to meet tough short- and long-term objectives are exactly what make a great State Farm agent.

When you work with a leading company that hosts nearly 100 individual products – including Auto, Home, Life and Financial Services – success may be achieved at an accelerated rate. That's the strength of State Farm and your strength for continuing your service career.

Family Comes First Here

The State Farm agency opportunity gives you the freedom to set your hours, your work/life balance and your level of success. That means you can be near your family as you serve in the neighborhood and community where you put down roots.

  • Serve in the neighborhood where you live
  • Put down solid roots
  • Always be near
  • Decide your own work/life balance
  • Have options to keep your lifestyle flexible
