Summer Internship FAQs

What are the dates for the 2016 Corporate College Summer Internship Program?

The summer 2016 11-week program is from May 23, 2016 through August 5, 2016.

Are the interns provided housing with their internship?

Only corporate-based interns are eligible to choose housing arranged by State Farm. This year's housing includes locations at The Oaks Luxury Townhomes, 1010 Lofts, and Uptown North.

Rent and utilities are $1550 per intern for the summer. The $1550 housing expense is billed and paid directly by State Farm Insurance. However, income and Social Security taxes are withheld from the intern's paycheck because it is considered taxable income and is reported as additional wages.

Note: Individuals staying at a First Site property must provide a $200 refundable security deposit on move-in day, May 21, 2016. All deposits must be a check or money order, no cash is accepted.

Hub-based interns are eligible for our Summer Internship Housing Stipend program, and are responsible for locating suitable housing for the summer.

Who is eligible for the Summer Internship Housing Stipend program?

All summer interns at one of the designated hub locations are eligible for the housing stipend.

Hub locations include:
  • Dallas
  • Atlanta
  • Phoenix

Are there tax implications with the summer internship housing stipend?

Yes. The housing stipend has tax implications. It is reported as additional wages and both income and Social Security taxes are withheld from the intern's paycheck.

How will the summer internship housing stipend be paid?

Summer interns based in hub locations will receive a lump sum payment approximately two weeks from the first day the intern reports to work.

How is housing secured for summer interns receiving the intern housing stipend?

Housing accommodations will not be secured for any summer interns located in hub locations. Summer interns will receive a stipend amount to secure housing for the summer.

Is there a list of housing locations to assist non-local interns?

Yes. There is a housing list for non-local summer interns at each hub location.

Note: State Farm does not endorse any of the listed locations.

Are interns eligible for relocation?

No. Summer interns are considered "benefits ineligible." Therefore, summer interns are not entitled to any relocation benefits.

Are interns eligible for reimbursement on travel expenses incurred from their summer internship?

Yes. Interns who travel over 50 miles to Bloomington are eligible to receive travel expense reimbursements. All travel reimbursement requests need to be discussed with recruiting and the hiring manager during the interviewing process.

Are interns eligible to receive insurance benefits?

Summer interns are considered "part-time ineligible" and are therefore not eligible for group insurance benefits.

However, interns are eligible to participate in:
  • 401K contributions
  • Purchasing mutual funds
  • Lifeworks
  • State Farm Park
  • The discount program

Are interns eligible for the 401(k) Savings Plan?

All interns are eligible to participate in the State Farm 401(k) Savings Plan. Interns are automatically enrolled in the 401(k) Savings Plan and have 30 days from their start date to opt-out or change the contribution amount. The default contribution is 3% of an intern's salary.

State Farm does not provide a matching contribution or non-elective contribution for summer interns. Interns have the following options at the end of their contract:
  • Rollover the 401(k) into an IRA or other qualifying plan
  • Cash out the 401(k) and receive a lump sum (subject to taxes and penalties)

Are interns eligible to open a State Farm Federal Credit Union® account?

No. Interns are not eligible for this benefit. State Farm Federal Credit Union members must be employed by State Farm for at least five consecutive months before they can open an account.

Are interns eligible to participate in the van pool program?

No. The van pool fare is for a six-month period. Therefore, interns are not eligible to participate.

Are interns eligible to reserve a company car?

Yes. Interns should follow the standard procedures to request and reserve a company car. Company cars are for business use only and must be approved by management.

Are interns eligible to apply for the Good Neighbor Grant®?

Yes. If an intern volunteers at least 40 hours to a qualifying non-profit organization during the internship, they can apply for a $500 Good Neighbor Grant. Volunteer hours must be completed within the internship period at State Farm.

Volunteer service can include: 
  • Board service
  • Committee work
  • Fundraising

Interns cannot earn hours toward the Good Neighbor Grant when they are in paid status (e.g., hours that IWU ARC interns work at their non-profits do not qualify). Participation in one-time events, such as walk-a-thons, are also not eligible towards the Good Neighbor Grant.

Are interns eligible for the Matching Gift Program?

Yes. All donations must be made during the internship period at State Farm to qualify for the Matching Gift Program.

I have questions about how to apply.

Visit our Job Application Help page for additional assistance.
